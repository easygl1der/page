Numerical Solution Research of Lane-Emden-Fowler Equations

Mathematica Course Project

📋 Project Abstract

Lane-Emden-Fowler equations are a class of second-order nonlinear differential equations with wide applications in mathematical physics and numerous engineering fields, such as astrophysics, fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, etc., which can be used to describe phenomena such as stellar structure and heat conduction. To solve these equations, mathematicians have used a large number of analytical methods, such as Kummer-Liouville transformations, Lie symmetries, and Painlevé analysis, and some have used analytical mechanics methods to solve them. At the same time, since these differential equations do not have exact solutions in the general sense, mathematicians have also developed many numerical solution methods. Furthermore, by introducing fractional derivatives, time-fractional Emden-Fowler equations are more suitable for some problems in astronomy and mathematical physics, although they are also more complex. Current research mainly adopts Elzaki transform homotopy perturbation method (ET-HPM) and Laplace transform homotopy perturbation method (LH-HPM) to provide analytical solutions. This method combines perturbation techniques to obtain numerical methods for solving such equations. In solving specific problems, Mathematica's powerful computational and graphical capabilities play an important role, including solving equations and plotting solution images. However, due to the excessive computational load of some equations, Mathematica cannot produce results. I solved some of these problems, but there are still some computational problems that cannot be resolved.

🔬 Research Methods

  • Elzaki Transform Homotopy Perturbation Method (ET-HPM) - Combining Elzaki transform with homotopy perturbation method
  • Laplace Transform Homotopy Perturbation Method (LH-HPM) - Combining Laplace transform with homotopy perturbation method
  • Fractional Derivative Extension - Extending equations to time-fractional Emden-Fowler equations
  • Numerical Computation and Visualization - Using Mathematica for equation solving and result visualization

⚡ Technical Challenges

During project implementation, several technical challenges were encountered:

  • Excessive computational load of some complex equations exceeded Mathematica's computational capacity
  • Need to balance the accuracy of analytical solutions with the computational efficiency of numerical methods
  • Introduction of fractional derivatives increased equation complexity
  • Visualizing complex solution images required algorithm and parameter optimization
